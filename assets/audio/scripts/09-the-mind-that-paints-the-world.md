The Mind That Paints the World

[pause 3s]

Welcome.

[pause]

Take one slow breath.

[inhale]

[pause]

And gently breathe out.

[exhale]

[pause 5s]

Look around the room you're in.

Perhaps there is a chair.

A table.

A window.

A cup.

[pause]

Notice something interesting.

Your mind sees these objects...

and almost immediately...

gives them names.

Chair.

Table.

Cup.

Window.

[pause]

Then...

it moves on.

There is little emotion.

Little attachment.

[pause 8s]

Now imagine someone places a diamond...

on that same table.

[pause]

Suddenly...

the mind becomes interested.

It says...

"That's valuable."

"I must protect it."

"I wish it belonged to me."

[pause]

The diamond has not spoken.

It has not asked to be owned.

Only the mind has changed.

[pause 10s]

Now imagine meeting someone very attractive.

Notice what happens.

Perhaps admiration.

Perhaps excitement.

Perhaps longing.

Perhaps comparison.

[pause]

Again...

the person has done nothing.

Your mind has begun painting a story.

[pause 10s]

Our experience of the world...

is rarely just the world.

It is the world...

plus the stories...

the memories...

the fears...

the desires...

that the mind quietly adds.

[pause 10s]

Imagine wearing glasses with blue lenses.

Every tree looks blue.

Every house looks blue.

Eventually...

you forget you're wearing the glasses.

You believe the whole world is blue.

[pause]

The mind works in much the same way.

It colors every experience.

[pause 10s]

When we become aware of this...

something extraordinary happens.

We begin seeing...

not only the object...

but also the reaction.

[pause]

The object.

And the story.

[pause]

The person.

And the desire.

[pause]

The criticism.

And the hurt.

[pause]

The success.

And the pride.

[pause 10s]

Once you notice the story...

you no longer have to become it.

[pause]

A thought appears.

You simply see it.

[pause]

A feeling appears.

You simply notice it.

[pause]

A desire appears.

You don't have to chase it.

[pause]

Like clouds crossing a quiet sky.

[pause 12s]

For a moment...

look at your own breath.

[pause]

Your mind doesn't call it expensive.

Or beautiful.

Or successful.

It is simply...

a breath.

[pause]

Because there is no story...

there is peace.

[pause 10s]

Now gently ask yourself...

How many things today...

have I been seeing clearly...

and how many have I been seeing...

through stories created by my own mind?

[pause]

No need to answer.

Just notice.

[pause 12s]

Every moment of awareness...

removes a little color from the glasses.

A little more reality appears.

A little more freedom arrives.

[pause]

The world becomes lighter...

not because it changed...

but because the mind became quieter.

[pause 12s]

Take one final slow breath.

[inhale]

[pause]

And gently breathe out.

[exhale]

[pause]

As you open your eyes...

remember this.

The world is often simpler...

than the stories we tell about it.

And every time you notice the story...

without becoming lost inside it...

your mind becomes a little quieter...

a little lighter...

and a little more free.

[pause 4s]

May you see clearly.

May you respond wisely.

May your mind rest peacefully...
just as it is.
