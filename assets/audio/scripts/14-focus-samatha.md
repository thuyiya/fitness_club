Focus

Steady, One-Pointed Attention

[pause]

Welcome.

[pause]

This is a practice of gathering.

Not forcing.

Not straining.

[pause]

Throughout the day...

the mind scatters...

into a hundred small pieces.

[pause]

For the next few minutes...

we'll gently bring it back...

to a single, quiet point.

[pause]

Like a still candle flame...

that no longer flickers.

[pause 3s]

Let your body settle.

[pause]

Sit in a way that feels...

both relaxed...

and awake.

[pause]

Let your hands rest.

Let your shoulders soften.

[pause]

...

...

[exhale]

[pause 4s]

Now...

allow the breath...

to return to its own natural rhythm.

[pause]

Nothing to change.

Nothing to control.

[pause]

Simply find the place...

where you feel the breath most clearly.

[pause]

Perhaps the tip of the nose.

Perhaps the cool air...

at the edge of the nostrils.

[pause]

Choose one small spot...

and let your attention rest there.

[pause 4s]

This single spot...

is your anchor.

[pause]

Rest your awareness on it...

as lightly...

as a feather...

landing on water.

[pause]

Breathing in...

you know the breath is coming in.

[pause]

Breathing out...

you know the breath is leaving.

[pause 5s]

To steady the mind...

let's use a gentle count.

[pause]

With each out-breath...

silently count.

[pause]

Breathe in...

and out...

one.

In...

two.

In...

three.

[pause]

Continue on your own...

all the way to ten.

[pause]

And when you reach ten...

simply begin again at one.

[pause 12s]

If you lose the count...

that's perfectly okay.

[pause]

There is no failure here.

Simply return...

to one.

[pause]

Each return...

is the practice.

[pause]

A thought will carry you away.

A sound.

A memory.

A plan.

[pause]

[pause]

Gently...

A little stronger.

[pause]

Let your attention become subtle too.

[pause]
