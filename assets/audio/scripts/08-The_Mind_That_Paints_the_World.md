The Mind That Paints the World

[pause 6s]

Welcome.

Allow yourself to arrive exactly as you are.

There is nothing to force.

Nothing to solve.

Only this quiet moment.

[pause 10s]

Take a slow breath in.

[inhale]

...

And gently breathe out.

[exhale]

[pause 12s]

Look around the room.

Notice the shapes.

The colors.

The light.

Without rushing to give anything a name.

Simply notice.

[pause 15s]

Very quickly, the mind says...

Chair.

Window.

Table.

Cup.

Naming is natural.

But after naming...

the mind begins adding something more.

Opinions.

Memories.

Likes.

Dislikes.

Little stories.

[pause 15s]

Imagine someone placing a beautiful diamond on the table.

Nothing about the room has changed very much.

Yet suddenly the mind becomes busy.

"It is valuable."

"I hope it is safe."

"I wish it were mine."

Notice carefully.

The object is still resting quietly.

Only the mind has become active.

[pause 18s]

This happens throughout the day.

We meet a person.

A sound.

A message.

A memory.

Before long...

the mind paints each experience with its own colors.

[pause 15s]

For a few moments...

rest with the breath.

Notice each inhale arriving.

Each exhale leaving.

The breath asks for nothing.

It simply comes...

and goes.

[silence]

[pause 35s]

Silently repeat...

May I see clearly.

[pause 6s]

May I notice without rushing to judge.

[pause 8s]

May my heart remain calm.

[pause 10s]

Imagine wearing colored glasses every day.

After a while...

you forget they are there.

You believe the world itself has changed.

Our thoughts often work this way.

We mistake our stories...

for reality.

[pause 15s]

Perhaps someone speaks only a few words.

The mind quickly decides...

"They don't like me."

"They are disappointed."

"I have done something wrong."

Pause.

Were those facts...

or were they thoughts?

[pause 18s]

Notice how often the mind finishes unfinished stories.

It fills empty spaces.

It predicts tomorrow.

It revisits yesterday.

Yet life is always happening...

here.

In this breath.

In this sound.

In this moment.

[pause 18s]

The more quietly we observe...

the more we discover that thoughts appear by themselves.

Feelings arise.

Then fade.

Like waves reaching the shore.

None of them stays forever.

[pause 18s]

Imagine sitting beside a peaceful river.

Leaves float past on the water.

You do not jump in after every leaf.

You simply watch.

Thoughts can be observed in the same gentle way.

Seen.

Accepted.

Allowed to pass.

[pause 20s]

Rest quietly.

[silence]

[pause 45s]

Silently repeat...

May I meet each moment with fresh eyes.

[pause 6s]

May I let go of unnecessary stories.

[pause 6s]

May wisdom grow through quiet attention.

[pause 10s]

Take one final slow breath in.

[inhale]

...

And gently breathe out.

[exhale]

[pause 10s]

As you continue your day...

remember that peace is not found by controlling every thought.

It is found by seeing thoughts for what they are...

passing visitors in a spacious mind.

The clearer your awareness becomes...

the lighter your heart feels.

May you see with clarity.

May you respond with kindness.

May your mind rest in quiet freedom.
